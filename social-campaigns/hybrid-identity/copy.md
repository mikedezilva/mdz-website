## 🔵 LinkedIn Post (PDF Carousel)

The industry loves to put creators in boxes. 📦

"You're either a creative, or you're a tech person. Pick a lane."

I spent 13 years in creative advertising and media sales in Sri Lanka, surrounded by music. But when I started learning software development, blockchain, and AI, I refused to choose a lane. 

I didn't learn tech for a new job title. I learned it to build tools that protect and distribute the art we were already creating.

And here is the most surprising thing I realized once I started coding:

Writing software is almost identical to arranging a song. 

In music, you take modular stems—drums, bass, vocals—and organize them so they work together as a single, cohesive track. Writing software is exactly the same: building modular components and APIs, ensuring they talk to each other without breaking. 

When I am coding, I am arranging.
When I am mixing, I am organizing logic.

If you are a developer who plays in a band, or a designer who wants to deploy smart contracts, do not buy into the idea that you have to hide one side of yourself to be taken seriously. 

The most interesting ideas happen at the intersections. 🚀
